What Is Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1?

Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 is the official entry-level graded examination offered by Rockschool Limited (RSL), one of the UK's leading contemporary music awarding bodies. It is the very first rung on a structured ladder that takes guitarists all the way from beginner level to Grade 8 and beyond. The grade 1 syllabus is carefully designed to introduce students to the fundamental building blocks of electric guitar playing in a fun, rock-music context — making it far more engaging than traditional classical exams for most students.

Whether you are a student, a parent looking for a structured learning path for your child, or an adult beginner hoping to formalise your guitar practice, Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 provides a globally recognised qualification that validates your skills and motivates continued progress. The exam is taken in front of an accredited RSL examiner, either in-person at an approved centre or via digital submission, and results are issued with written feedback so you always know where to improve. For students looking for expert-guided preparation, Gandharva School Of Music offers dedicated electric guitar courses tailored to the Rockschool syllabus, helping students achieve distinction-level results with structured, professional tuition.

Rockschool Grade 1 Songs & Pieces

The Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 syllabus includes a selection of specially arranged performance pieces drawn from rock, pop, blues, and classic guitar styles. Students select three pieces to perform in the exam — one from each of three stylistic groups (A, B, and C). The songs are chosen to test a range of skills including rhythm playing, basic lead lines, dynamics, and stylistic authenticity.

Typical songs in the Grade 1 book span genres that will be familiar and motivating for beginners. These pieces are arranged specifically for Grade 1 level, meaning they are accessible without being simplistic — they teach real musical concepts while remaining achievable with a few months of consistent practice.

Essential Techniques for Grade 1

Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 is built around a clearly defined set of techniques that every student must demonstrate. These techniques appear across the performance pieces, technical exercises, and are assessed implicitly throughout the exam. Understanding what the examiner is looking for in each technique is the key to achieving Merit or Distinction.

Technical Exercises Component

In addition to the performance pieces, Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 includes a dedicated technical exercises section worth 15 marks. The examiner will ask you to play one exercise from a selection, which could include scales (E minor pentatonic, E natural minor, or E major), arpeggios, or chord sequences. This section rewards students who have consistently drilled the fundamentals rather than focusing only on their songs.

Music Knowledge & Ear Tests

Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 is not just a practical exam — it also assesses your understanding of music theory and your ability to recognise what you hear. Both components are worth marks and both can be prepared for systematically.

Music Knowledge (10 Marks)

The examiner will ask you questions about the music you have performed. At Grade 1, these questions cover topics such as: note names on the guitar (open strings and first position), basic rhythm values (crotchets, quavers, minims, semibreves), time signatures (particularly 4/4 and 3/4), tempo markings, and simple dynamic markings (piano, forte). You do not need to read standard notation fluently — understanding TAB and basic theory is sufficient.

Ear Tests (15 Marks)

The ear test component at Grade 1 involves listening to a short musical passage played by the examiner (or a recording) and responding to questions about it. Tasks typically include: identifying whether a short melody goes up or down (melodic direction), clapping or tapping back a simple rhythm (rhythmic recall), and recognising whether a two-bar phrase is played the same or differently the second time around. These skills are developed through active, attentive listening during every practice session.

  • Sing along to backing tracks — ear training happens through active listening, not passive hearing.
  • Use call-and-response: play a two-bar phrase, then try to copy it by ear without looking at the TAB.
  • Clap rhythms from the Grade 1 pieces before playing them — rhythm comprehension is half the battle.
  • Study the RSL Music Knowledge Workbook for Grade 1 theory questions in a structured format.

Exam Day Tips for Rockschool Grade 1

Being well-prepared technically is only part of passing Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 with a high score. What separates a Pass from a Distinction is often how the student manages themselves on exam day. Here is what experienced guitar teachers recommend to students sitting their first Rockschool grade.

  • Tune up before you enter the room. Your guitar must be in tune at the start of the exam. Use a clip-on tuner or the tuning function on your amp. An out-of-tune performance immediately costs marks regardless of technique.
  • Choose your three strongest pieces. Do not perform a piece you are only 80% confident in. Play to your strengths — examiners reward confident, musical performance over technically ambitious but shaky attempts.
  • Start at a tempo you can sustain. Many students rush under exam nerves. If a piece goes wrong, it is almost always because the tempo was too fast at the start. Set a comfortable, musical tempo and maintain it.
  • Use dynamics intentionally. One of the most effective ways to pick up bonus marks at Grade 1 is to play with clear dynamic contrast — louder sections feel louder, quieter sections feel quieter. This shows musical awareness.
  • Stay calm after a mistake. If you make an error, keep going. Examiners are trained to look at the overall performance, not individual moments. A graceful recovery scores far better than stopping and restarting.
  • Know your music theory answers in advance. The knowledge round takes only 2–3 minutes. Practice answering likely questions out loud — "What is the time signature?" "Name this note" — so you feel natural and confident.
  • Do a mock exam at least twice before the real one. Simulate full exam conditions: play all three pieces back-to-back, do the technical exercises, and have someone ask you the knowledge questions. This removes the element of surprise on the day.

Recommended Practice Schedule

How long does it take to prepare for Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1? Most students with no prior experience reach exam-ready standard in 6 to 12 months of regular, structured practice. Students who have played casually for a year or more may be ready in 3 to 6 months. The quality of practice matters far more than the quantity — 30 focused minutes daily outperforms 3 hours of unfocused playing every week.

🗓️

Week 1–4

Learn open chords and basic strumming. Introduce the E minor pentatonic scale. Choose your 3 pieces.

📈

Week 5–12

Work through all 3 pieces section by section. Begin technical exercises. Start ear training actively.

🎯

Week 13–20

Polish all pieces to performance level. Run full mock exams. Lock in music knowledge answers.

🏁

Final Week

Light practice only. Rest your hands. Tune your guitar the night before. Trust your preparation.

What Comes After Grade 1?

Completing Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 is just the beginning. The RSL graded music exam framework runs from Debut (pre-Grade 1) all the way through to Grade 8 and the Performance Certificates. Each grade builds systematically on the last, adding new techniques, more complex pieces, and deeper music theory knowledge.

After Grade 1, students move on to Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 2, where they encounter more advanced chord work (including barre chords), extended scale patterns, more demanding ear tests, and pieces drawn from a wider range of rock genres. Many students also begin to explore improvisation and songwriting at this level, giving the graded path a genuinely creative dimension.

Grades 3 to 5 form the intermediate level, and Grades 6 to 8 are considered advanced — equivalent to A-level standard and recognised by UCAS for university points. For anyone who wants to study music at degree level, progressing through the Rockschool system provides both the skills and the formal qualifications to do so.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to read music to take Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1?
No. Rockschool uses guitar TAB notation alongside standard notation, so you can prepare for and pass Grade 1 without being able to read traditional sheet music. However, basic music theory knowledge — rhythm values, time signatures — is assessed in the knowledge round.
What guitar do I need for Rockschool Grade 1?
Any standard electric guitar in good condition will work. You do not need an expensive instrument. You will need access to a working amplifier during the exam. Ensure your guitar is properly set up — low action, fresh strings, and in tune — as these factors significantly affect how easy the exam feels to play.
Can adults take Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1?
Absolutely. There is no age limit for Rockschool graded exams. Many adult beginners find the grade 1 exam to be a brilliant motivational tool — it provides a clear goal and a formal recognition of their progress that makes the often-challenging early months of learning guitar feel rewarding and structured.
How much does the Rockschool Grade 1 exam cost?
Exam fees vary by country and exam centre. In the UK, Rockschool Grade 1 fees are typically in the range of £30–£50. Check the official RSL website or your local registered exam centre for current pricing in your region.
Where can I find the official Rockschool Grade 1 book?
The official Rockschool Electric Guitar Grade 1 book is available from RSL's official website, Amazon, and most music retailers worldwide. It includes all available pieces with TAB and notation, plus backing tracks accessed via a unique download code included in the book.
Is Rockschool Grade 1 recognised for school qualifications?
Yes. Rockschool grades are regulated by Ofqual in England and are equivalent to national curriculum levels. Grade 1 does not carry UCAS points, but it is recognised as a formal instrumental grade for school music reports and scholarships at many independent schools.
